The History of Cats

From ancient desert predators to revered gods, seafaring mousers, and internet royalty — the extraordinary 10,000-year journey of humanity's most enigmatic companion.

How It All Began

The domestic cat's journey from wild hunter to global companion spans thousands of years of mutual benefit, reverence, and cohabitation with humans.

🌾

Fertile Crescent Origins

Around 7500 BCE, wildcats in the Near East began hunting rodents attracted to grain stores in early farming settlements. This mutually beneficial relationship sparked domestication.

🏟

Sacred in Egypt

Ancient Egyptians revered cats as sacred animals. The goddess Bastet, depicted as a cat, was worshipped widely. Killing a cat, even accidentally, was punishable by death.

🌎

Global Companions

Today cats live on every continent except Antarctica. With over 600 million domestic cats worldwide, they are one of the most successful species on Earth.

Cats Through Time

Key moments in the history of cats and humans.

~7500 BCE

First Domestication

Wildcats (Felis silvestris lybica) begin living alongside humans in the Fertile Crescent, drawn by rodents feeding on stored grain.

~3000 BCE

Cats in Ancient Egypt

Cats become deeply embedded in Egyptian society and religion. They are mummified, mourned, and depicted in art alongside pharaohs and gods.

~500 BCE

Spread to Asia & Europe

Phoenician traders and Roman soldiers carry cats across the Mediterranean. Cats reach China, India, and eventually northern Europe.

Middle Ages

Superstition & Persecution

In medieval Europe, cats — especially black ones — become associated with witchcraft and are persecuted. Ironically, their decline may have worsened the plague by allowing rat populations to surge.

1871

First Cat Show

The world's first major cat show is held at the Crystal Palace in London, organized by Harrison Weir. It sparks global interest in cat breeding and fancy.

1960s

Rise of Indoor Cats

The invention of commercial cat litter by Edward Lowe transforms cats from outdoor mousers into beloved indoor pets, reshaping the human-cat relationship.

2000s

Internet Cat Domination

Cats become the undisputed rulers of the internet. From LOLCats to Grumpy Cat, feline content generates billions of views and launches a new era of cat celebrity.

Cats in Pop Culture

Cats have clawed their way into every corner of modern life.

Internet Royalty

Grumpy Cat, Lil Bub, and Nyan Cat became global sensations. Cat videos are among the most-watched content online, with billions of cumulative views.

Literary Cats

From the Cheshire Cat in Alice in Wonderland to T.S. Eliot's "Old Possum's Book of Practical Cats" (which inspired the musical CATS), felines have captivated authors for centuries.

Studio Ghibli & Anime

Japanese animation celebrates cats endlessly — from "The Cat Returns" and the Catbus in "My Neighbor Totoro" to the beckoning Maneki-neko figures found worldwide.

Cat Cafes

Originating in Taiwan in 1998 and popularized in Japan, cat cafes have spread globally, offering people a chance to relax with feline companions over coffee.

Famous Ship Cats

Cats have sailed on ships for thousands of years as mousers. Unsinkable Sam survived three shipwrecks in WWII, and Trim circumnavigated Australia with Matthew Flinders.

Space Cat

In 1963, France launched Félicette into space, making her the first and only cat to travel beyond Earth's atmosphere. She returned safely and became a national icon.
